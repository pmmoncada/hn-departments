Deportation

One of the largest headlines about Honduras is about illegal immigration. This trend is not recent and it is important to be able to target the areas in which this occurs the most and the cause behind it.

Get Started

The biggest reason people look to immigrate is because of uneployment

Many of the immigrants that get sent back to Honduras claim that all they were looking for was more opportunities to earn more money and provide a better future for themselves and for their families bellow you can see a graph that shows the years with the highest unemployment rates in Honduras, the data were obtained from a report published by The World Bank. 2014 stands out the most.

Click on this link for access to more data from The World Bank.

Other factors that are pushing people to migrate:

  • Insecurity Issues in Honduras
  • Corruption
  • Droughts, floods, and other phenomena linked to climate change
  • Intolerable home situations, there are high levels of domestic violence reported in Honduras


    • What can the Honduran Government do?

    Governments can’t prevent citizens from leaving Honduras nor prevent them from peacefully assembling and marching away. Doing so could be a fundamental violation of citizens’ rights and internationally recognized human rights. Also, the costs to mobilize any troops to defend blind stops on the borders is very controversial, and would be ineffective with substantial costs involved.
